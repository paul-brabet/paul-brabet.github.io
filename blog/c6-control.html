Control

2 April 2017

When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?
Reflecting on the week gone by, I don't think there was a moment of disproportionate emotional response. At no time was I particularly frustrated or outraged. I think the more I practise coding, the more aware I am of my limitations, and so I become more accepting when things go wrong.
How did you find the Siberian North Railroad (SNR) exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?
I feel like it is an exercise I regularly do. For all my flaws, I consider myself someone who stays unusually calm when dealing with people.
However, maybe this is something I don't exercise when I am dealing with an aggravating situation that doesn't involve other people, e.g. a computer that crashes, burnt food, or being late for the bus. I have never felt an incentive to de-escalate or use the NSR exercise in these situations, because if I am angry at something inanimate, who does it hurt? After reflecting on chapter 5 I am beginning to think that I'm doing myself no favours by feeding or stewing in anger on these issues, and there lies an area where I can use the SNR exercise more often.
